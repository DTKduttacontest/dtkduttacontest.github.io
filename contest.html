Aims of the Contest

Bhagalpur, once among the largest cities in India, has had much to boast through the centuries, from distant antiquities to relatively modern days. However, much of the youth of this city, overwhelmed by the infrastructural insufficiencies and the lack of opportunities, have either resigned themselves to inaction or are desperately seeking ways to leave it behind. To reconnect the youth to their heritage and the city, the DTK Dutta Contest is a platform for them to explore the past and present of Bhagalpur.

This competition is designed to give secondary and higher secondary school students a taste of research while exploring the city’s socio-economic and cultural milieu. Every iteration of the competition will have a theme focusing on a specific aspect of Bhagalpur. Over a period of six months, the participating teams will have to come up with their own questions, develop methodologies to investigate those questions, and present their findings. Our aim is to help students get a new perspective on the social, urban, linguistic, historical, cultural, and ecological features of the city as they experience it through scholarly investigation and develop valuable skills for their future.

Timeline and Details

The contest would take place in three phases:

Phase 1: Participant Selection (April) - Initially, ten students will be nominated by their school or selected through a preliminary quiz. They will submit a short write-up related to the contest theme, from which four students will be chosen to represent their school based on the originality, insight, and thoughtfulness of their work.

Phase 2: Project Specification (May-July) - In this stage, participants build their knowledge base and refine their research questions within the competition’s theme. Working with a mentoring committee, students discuss ideas, receive feedback, and narrow their focus to a feasible project while connecting with experts and resources for later stages.

Phase 3: Execution & Submission (August-November) - Once a team’s research question and methodology are approved, it proceeds to the execution phase. Guided by their proposal and evaluated by a judging committee—including one mentor and three additional judges—teams carry out their projects while carefully citing all sources used.
